# Cohesive Ranked List of All Cattle Futures Spreads

## Tier 1 — Primary Calendar Spreads (the report's analytical backbone)

* **April/June LE (LEJ/LEM)** — Called "arguably the most critical spread in the Live Cattle complex." Gets its own signal table, specific 2026 settlement data ($258.475 vs. $253.00), weekly move ($7.77), and is the arbiter of whether the spring peak is on schedule or delayed. Shadow Convenience Yield. Appears in Sections 4.2, 8.5, 10.3, and 13.1.
* **June/August LE (LEM/LEQ)** — Primary barometer for the summer beef market. Carries specific 2026 spread value ($5.075 premium), carcass weight interaction (981–989 lbs), and is the dominant roll-window spread (GSCI Jun→Aug bull-spreading). Appears in Sections 4.3, 8.1, 8.5, 9.1, and 13.1.
* **August/December LE Skip-Month (LEQ/LEZ)** — The report's single most important regime-change indicator. Two distinct 2026 data points ($5.125 expanding to $6.20 inversion), called "the most reliable indicator of a potential regime change in the second half." Shadow Convenience Yield. Appears in Sections 4.7, 13.1, and 14.
* **August/October LE (LEQ/LEV)** — The "fall run" barometer. Running well above 5-year high in 2026. Carries Darin Newsom attribution on broadening formations as regime-change markers. Appears in Sections 4.4, 8.5, 9.1, and 10.3.
* **Cattle Crush (LE–GF–ZC)** — The inter-commodity synthetic spread measuring feedlot profitability. Full formula treatment (8-4-2 and 1-1-2 ratios), historical benchmark (-$2,970 in March 2023), volatility spillover analysis, and stocker operator decision framework. Entire Section 6 dedicated to it.
* **Choice-Select Spread** — Not a calendar spread but the report's most-cited companion diagnostic. Full time series (April 13–May 4), the 2026 inversion to -$1.50, record-low 7.9% Select gradeout. Entire Section 7.1 plus appearances in 13.4 and 14.

## Tier 2 — High-Importance Calendar Spreads (full standalone sections with 2026 context)

* **February/April LE (LEG/LEJ)** — Winter-to-spring transition diagnostic. Full Section 4.1 treatment covering inversion signals and forward inference.
* **October/December LE (LEV/LEZ)** — Fall low vs. holiday recovery. Inversion signal described as "powerful" for near-term scarcity. Section 4.5.
* **December/February LE (LEZ/LEG)** — Year-end close of the annual LE cycle. Described as the "P&L driver on the roll" for hedgers. Section 4.6.
* **January/March GF (GFF/GFH)** — The "grass fever" diagnostic. Carries specific 2026 data (Feeder Cattle Index at $377.37 historic high). Section 5.1.
* **November/January GF (GFX/GFF)** — Designated the "missing link" that closes the feeder cattle annual cycle. Specific 2026 data ($365.83 vs. $359.90, $5.93 November premium). Section 5.6.
* **May/August GF (GFK/GFQ)** — Cross-seasonal spread bridging spring placement to late-summer yearling run. Specific 2026 data showing anomalous near-parity ($371.40 vs. $372.175). Section 5.3.
* **October/November GF (GFV/GFX)** — Peak fall run signal. Specific 2026 data (~$3.00 November discount). Section 5.5.
